Welcome to the end game app of crypto. Why Velvet? Ever since we were in crypto, people kept saying that the inherent technology behind blockchain is like IPV4 or IPV6, that blockchain is just a protocol and that eventually it would be used as a backend and people won't notice which blockchain they're on as long as they are transacting. 5 years later, and teams still don't think this way.Crypto for retailers is hard. Period.

Imagine telling a 50 year old investor that he needs to download an application called metamask, buy some ethereums on coinbase.com, copy an address with weird numbers on it from metamask, paste that into the coinbase send application, then wait till it's credited, then head to uniswap.com, set slippage, copy the address of the token he wants to buy, paste it and then hit on buy.Then this person needs to configure gas...
Shall we continue? And that's just the best case scenario. How many times you degens have tried to buy a token on uniswap, only to be frontran by mevs bots,slippage failing. Add to that the horrendous uniswap ui that doesn't refresh price feeds whenever price changes.
This person has absolutely no idea what gas is and nor should he. People assume that everyone is a crypto native and understands the lingo.
The hard red pill is that people are lazy. No one will change that.

The biggest obstacle to crypto adoption is the scene we described above.
That's the biggest problem with teams in crypto today : they're incredibly smart.
One might argue maybe a bit "too smart" for the space's own good. For crypto adoption to happen, this mindset needs to change.
Let's put ourselves in the shoes of the average retailer for once. This is exactly what we did with Velvet.

Our main products

----------------------

INNOVATION

Recently, people in crypto started thinking outside the box for a change.
We're talking about telegram bots. Telegram bots might seems ridiculous to some, but they are a huge undervalued innovation in crypto.
It allowed the average user to transact easily on a popoular application. Granted it's still a bit complicated, but this is normal. Innovation at the beginning is not perfect.

SMARTER

Without this innovation, Velvet would not be possible.
Immediately our team noticed the huge potential of this technology and we told ourselves: "Ok, this is great, how can we make it even betterand attractive to retailers?" Because in its current state, the bots are still quite complicated.
It needs to be configured, it also requires some complicated steps to access crypto. It also poses certain security threats if not treated as a hot wallet. This is where Velvet steps in

EASY TO USE

Velvet is the one stop application to do EVERYTHING in crypto with simple clicks. It's the all around app for any retailer that doesn't want to put the effort into researching the hows.
In a very transparent way, Velvet doesn't innovate anything, it just reshapes and makes it easier, which is what is needed.
The Velvet application will be an application where a user can buy crypto via a third party connected to the app with a credit card

INTEROPERABILITY

It will be a self custodial wallet, where the user can transact an buy any token available on a dex on ANY chain.
How is that possible? It's pretty simple. Velvet uses the bot technology inspired by Maestro / Unibot to do the transactions.
It also uses bots technology to bridge to any chain in the BACKEND. The user isn't aware of any of that.



100% completed

Simplicity at its best

Let's say a user wants to buy a Fantom token :
He choses the Fantom token from our aggregated tokens list (and if it's not there he has the option to copy paste the address), then clicks on buy.
That's all.
Our backend sends the amount of tokens he is swapping to a hot wallet controled by the bot. The bot then checks if it's on the main network or a different network, bridges if it's the latter, swaps and sends back the tokens to the user. As you can see, the user was not annoyed by slippage, bridging or gas. He just "bought".
